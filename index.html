Detected Spinal Diseases

1. Herniated Disc

Occurs when the soft center of a spinal disc pushes through a crack in the tougher exterior casing. It can irritate nearby nerves, causing pain, numbness, or weakness in limbs.

2. Spinal Stenosis

A narrowing of the spaces within your spine, which can put pressure on the nerves. Common symptoms include pain, tingling, and muscle weakness.

3. Scoliosis

A sideways curvature of the spine that occurs most often during the growth spurt before puberty. It can cause uneven shoulders and back pain.

4. Degenerative Disc Disease

Occurs when one or more of the discs between the vertebrae of the spinal column deteriorate or break down, leading to pain and reduced flexibility.

5. Spondylolisthesis

A condition in which one of the vertebrae slips out of place onto the vertebra below it, causing lower back pain and tight hamstrings.

6. Spinal Tumor

An abnormal growth within or surrounding the spinal cord and spinal column. Symptoms include persistent pain, weakness, and numbness.

7. Spinal Infection

Caused by bacteria or fungi infecting the spine, leading to fever, back pain, and fatigue. Early diagnosis is crucial for treatment.

8. Fracture

Breaks or cracks in the vertebrae often caused by trauma or osteoporosis. Symptoms include sudden pain and limited mobility.

9. Osteoarthritis

A degenerative joint disease that affects the cartilage and bones in the spine, leading to stiffness, pain, and swelling.

10. Ankylosing Spondylitis

A type of arthritis that causes inflammation of the spinal joints, leading to chronic pain, stiffness, and reduced flexibility.
